星を追う子ども

2011, 116 min.

Asuna Watase is a young daughter of a single mother, an exceptional student, and someone in need of distraction. She frequents her small hideout in a nearby mountain, and listens to an old crystal radio there. One day, she tuned the radio to a song of mystery and melancholy, one unlike anything she had ever heard.

Soon after, a boy named Shun saves her from a dangerous creature, and she becomes attached to him. He warns her to not return to the mountains, but she does, which leads to her being dragged into a long journey.

Her adventure is filled with everything from fantastical creatures, beautiful sights, and companionship to betrayal, grief, and longing.

This movie grapples with the theme of death as it relates to innocence very well, and feels like a genuine homage to Studio Ghibli.

Story: 8/10

Art: 9/10

Sound: 10/10

Characters: 8.5/10

Overall entertainment: 9/10

Total: 9/10

The art in this movie feels very stereotypically Ghibli, to the point where a lot of the creatures and characters feel like they were taken right out of Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind or Princess Mononoke. That being said, there are a lot of Shinkai-esque shots that remind you who the director here actually is. There are times where the animation feels a little weird, which is why a point is docked from art.

The soundtrack is a nice mix of light-hearted tracks, bittersweet orchestral tracks, and more, and the ending song "Hello, Goodbye and Hello" fits the story very well. The cast is strong overall, with believable characters all around - Asuna is a great example of how to introduce a kid that struggles with loneliness, grief, and maturity.

Overall, the movie is very good, but notably different from most of Shinkai's portfolio. There's a lighter, more adventurous tone present, which I personally found very entertaining given the style of the movie.
